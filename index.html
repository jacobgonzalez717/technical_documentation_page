Grammy Awards Explained

Awards and their meanings

  • Grammy Awards
    • People tend to know the name of these awards but don't understand the meaning therefore creating confusion. That's why we're here to briefly explain.


    What's the meaning behind the word Grammy?

    The Grammy Award (stylized as GRAMMY, originally called Gramophone Award), or just Grammy, is an award presented by the Recording Academy to recognize achievement in the music industry.

  • Album of the Year
    • Bruno Mars wins Album of the Year 2017 for "24K Magic"

    Who is awarded the Albumof the Year?

    is awarded to the performer, songwriter(s), and the production team of a full album if other than the performer.

  • Record of the Year
    • Adele wins Record of the Year 2017 for the song "Hello"

    Who is awarded the Record of the Year?

    is awarded to the performer and the production team of a single song if other than the performer.

  • Song of the Year
    • H.E.R wins Song of the Year 2021 for the song "I Can't Breathe "

    Who is awarded the Song of the Year?

    is awarded to the songwriter(s) of a single song.

  • Best New Artist
    • Billie Eilish wins best new artist 2020

    Who is awarded the Best New Artist?

    is awarded to a promising breakthrough performer who releases, during the Eligibility Year, the first recording that establishes the public identity of that artist (which is not necessarily their first proper release).
